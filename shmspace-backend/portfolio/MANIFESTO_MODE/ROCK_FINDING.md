Image is an inscribed black Ying scholar's rock, Qianlong 18th year, corresponding to 1753 and of the period. 11 in (28 cm) long. From [Christie's](https://www.christies.com/en/stories/collecting-guide-scholars-rocks-b39280a0d3ea4e5d81f4ad14eee2beb2)

my favorite form of found art is scholar's rocks / Suiseki / [Gōngshí](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gongshi) -- an art form that has been highly respected across several asian cultures for centuries -- where the goal is to find a REALLY GOOD ROCK !! 

you might also craft the rock a perfect stand to go with it, or write a poem about it, or modify it with a bit of carving.... 

but the focus  / driver of the piece is the Cool Rock you found!!

they even developed these deep frameworks and metrics for evaluating if a rock had [particularly interesting characteristics](https://www.arthistoryproject.com/subjects/scholars-stones/)... !! (like thinness (瘦 shòu), openness (透 tòu), or perforations (漏 lòu), ... and "resemblance" -- which was literally like, "how well does this rock express a particular vibe" e.g. "is this rock giving the vibe of a mountain or a waterfall"...)

I feel like Western art culture has a real emphasis on "making" / "crafting" but the emphasis in this art form is the skill of "finding"
Scholar's Rocks Creative Practitioners were respected for showcasing good finding skills, good taste... curation as creative practice!
so like

if [computational poetry is lost poetry](https://mkremins.github.io/publications/LostPoetry_HTTF2024.pdf) and computational art is lost art (wherein the computer takes on this role of "scrambling" or "rearranging" text/pixels/etc or otherwise, to present them in a novel way that artists can then "find")

then the AI / computational artform is basically... rock-finding

and this is why I have grown disinterested in making AI image generation feel like "painting" or "sketching" or other such forms of art-making (which we, as creativity support tool designers, are so frequently trying and struggling to do! but whenever I have tried to use AI image generation to replace direct-manipulation forms of making, it feels like I am working against the computational medium and what it wants to be...)

i now feel we need more AI art support systems that center curation/ support for art "finding"... 

TL;DR: the computational art tools that I want to make are rock gardens, quarries, with affordances for finding and collecting Cool Rocks, and for sorting, comparing, and sharing the Coolest Rocks you've found with others, for deeply engaging with, contemplating, and appreciating the (generated or historical or ???) artifacts we are able to produce and find through computational mediation...!
