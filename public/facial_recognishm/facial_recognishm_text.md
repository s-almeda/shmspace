i've been wondering what my face looks like.

recently, i've been (somewhat facetiously) wondering if computers might answer this question better than i can.

as a filipino kid in a very white town, from a young age i became increasingly aware that when people looked at me, they saw "an asian girl". we had a neighbor who would point at my sister and i and say "chinese"

these were things felt like they came outside, yet apparently i was carrying them around on my face, and i would stare in the mirror trying to see it

i've always really struggled with understanding faces — or rather, i think i get the sense that other people are doing this with more intuition than i do. facial recognition has felt like an onerously manual task. but like all issues i face i found a way to label this as a personal skill issue so i can avoid asking for help with it.

i got really, really, really into drawing faces. people told me that i was getting good, which i think means photorealistic. but i wanted to get good at understanding what other people were seeing when they looked at faces... especially mine.

in 4th grade my gym teacher mr. s gave me the nickname "smiley" and he told me to never lose that smile. i think it was notable that a lot of other kids were starting to hit that preteen time.

i think that sometimes you smile because you are happy, and sometimes you smile because you want others to know that you are still happy.
