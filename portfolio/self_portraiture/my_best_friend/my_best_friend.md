November 2019

it may seem strange that this is in self-portraiture, but I made a number of paintings with, and of, Hopper the rabbit as self-portraiture, and presented them in art courses where the assignment was self-portrature. i am very close with my familiar; i was exceptionally close with him during this time. we spent so much time together; i feel that our lives and identities as creatures on the planet were inextricable from one another at that point. 

in the time since, we've grown a bit apart.  we've been through many states (geographically, but also spiritually) and moved across the country together. 
 I have gone through multiple career changes and major relationship changes. he has gone through a major relationship change, too (i adopted another rabbit named Anya; they spend much more time with each other than Hopper spends with me, now). While hopper is still so so important to who Shm is, I've changed; expanded, such that creating a painting of Hopper and calling it a "self portrait" is to paint a portrait that kind of fails to capture my full "self" very well.

but i will always, always hold precious the lessons this rabbit has taught me about living.
