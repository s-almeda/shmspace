i suspect that my recurring fixation on mirrors, reflection, self-portraiture, and diarizing my outfits is probably mostly just self-obsession 

(i'm a really cool person. and i have to hang out with me all the time. can you blame me for being a bit obsessed.)

but i think that I am also frequently grappling with a bit of face-blindness. like, struggling to interpret others' faces, and my own. 

I think this is why *love* drawing people from observation and trying to capture their **Likeness**. It's a way of documenting, then checking, if I correctly identified the features that make a person Look Like Themselves. 

( [...did you know i was the first champion of the junior national mock trial courtroom sketch artist competition.](https://www.njsbf.org/news/977.html) )

Sometimes I really struggle, more than anyone else, to figure out what those qualities are *for Shm*. Maybe this is why I like having over-the-top iconographic features (green hair orange glasses colorful blazer rabbit and carrot motif etc.)

much of this self-portraiture (e.g. 2016-2019) is also from a time where I was constantly struggling with an impossible loaded space of questions like, 'what gender do i "look like"?'
'how much of a gender do i "look like" right now?'

I used to feel so assaulted by mirrors, in a sense. like i could not pass a mirror without instantly kickstarting this obsessive visual evaluation process in my brain, where it would frantically be trying to figure out if I was doing a gender and doing it right, trying to figure out what i could do better ...

these days, when i pass a mirror, i mostly just think, "ooh, look at that guy, they're cool!" 

I think self-portraiture, self-expression (e.g. fun outfits), and diarizing my self-expression has really helped a lot with this. 

if you struggle with Your Self and What It Looks Like, I recommend self-portraiture!

warning: it might make the struggle worse!! :D 
but i think it's productive. 

Oher people get to look at your form all the time. 

meaanwhile, all the time we spend with *ourselves* is spent *inside* our heads and looking outwards. and, if you're a visualizer, you might spend a lot of time visualizing what we *might* look like, and thereby distorting your visual understanding of your form. 

what a stark asymmetry that is, in terms of how we can interact time with ourselves vs. how others interact with us! no wonder we struggle to love ourselves the way others love us, to see our beauty the way others see it.

so I think it's worth trying to spend some time looking at your own form, if you find yourself scared or uncomfortable about it. then you can confront what it's like, and what parts of it are scary and uncomfortable for you. and you might find some parts that are unexpectedly fun and weird and nice, once you give them some consideration and honor. 

and once you have a way of capturing how you look, you can reduce that into a wacky little character design of yourself! and draw your little guy saying crazy things you want to say out loud, but can't! 
and now you're making autobiographical comics!
